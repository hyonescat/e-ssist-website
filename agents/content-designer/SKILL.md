# Content Designer Skill

## Humanizer: Remove AI Writing Patterns

When editing or reviewing text, remove signs of AI-generated writing to make it sound more natural and human. Based on Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing" guide.

### When to Humanize

- Final review of any copy before delivery
- User requests "make this sound more natural"
- Content feels sterile or robotic
- Need to match a specific human voice/tone

### The Process

1. **Identify AI patterns** - Scan for patterns listed below
2. **Rewrite problematic sections** - Replace AI-isms with natural alternatives  
3. **Preserve meaning** - Keep the core message intact
4. **Add soul** - Inject personality, opinions, varied rhythm
5. **Final anti-AI pass** - Ask: "What makes this obviously AI generated?" then fix it

---

## Patterns to Fix

### 1. Inflated Significance
**AI Pattern:** Overstating importance with grandiose language

**Watch for:** stands as, serves as, is a testament to, pivotal moment, crucial role, underscores the importance, broader movement, enduring legacy, deeply rooted

**Before:**
> The new feature was released, marking a pivotal moment in the evolution of our platform.

**After:**
> We shipped the new feature. People seem to like it.

---

### 2. Promotional/PR Language
**AI Pattern:** Sounds like marketing copy, not human speech

**Watch for:** excited to announce, thrilled to share, proud to introduce, groundbreaking, revolutionary, cutting-edge, best-in-class, industry-leading

**Before:**
> We are thrilled to announce our groundbreaking new partnership.

**After:**
> We teamed up with [company]. Should be interesting.

---

### 3. Vague -ing Analyses
**AI Pattern:** Superficial observations ending in -ing words

**Watch for:** highlighting, showcasing, demonstrating, emphasizing, reflecting, underscoring

**Before:**
> The data highlights the growing importance of user engagement.

**After:**
> User engagement is up. That's what matters.

---

### 4. Vague Attributions
**AI Pattern:** Passive, unclear who did what

**Watch for:** it has been noted, some argue, critics say, many believe, experts suggest

**Before:**
> It has been noted that the approach is effective.

**After:**
> The team found this approach works.

---

### 5. Em Dash Overuse
**AI Pattern:** Using em dashes excessively for emphasis

**Before:**
> The product—a revolutionary tool—is changing how we work.

**After:**
> The product is changing how we work. It's actually useful.

---

### 6. Rule of Three
**AI Pattern:** Lists of three that feel formulaic

**Before:**
> The platform is fast, reliable, and secure.

**After:**
> The platform is fast. And it hasn't crashed in months.

---

### 7. AI Vocabulary Words
**AI Pattern:** Words LLMs overuse

**Replace:**
- leverage → use
- utilize → use  
- facilitate → help/make easier
- optimize → improve
- robust → strong/solid
- seamless → smooth/easy
- holistic → complete/thorough
- paradigm → model/approach
- synergy → working together
- delve → look/explore

---

### 8. Negative Parallelisms
**AI Pattern:** "Not X, but Y" structures

**Before:**
> This is not just a tool, but a revolution.

**After:**
> This tool changes things.

---

### 9. Excessive Conjunctive Phrases
**AI Pattern:** Starting sentences with formal connectors

**Replace:**
- Furthermore → Also / Plus
- Moreover → And / Also
- Consequently → So
- Nevertheless → But / Still
- Subsequently → Then / Later
- In conclusion → So / Bottom line

---

## Adding Soul

Removing AI patterns isn't enough. Add humanity:

### Have Opinions
Don't just report - react.
- ❌ "The results were mixed."
- ✅ "I genuinely don't know how to feel about these results."

### Vary Rhythm
- Short sentences.
- Then longer ones that flow and breathe and take their time.
- Mix it up.

### Acknowledge Complexity
- ❌ "This is good."
- ✅ "This works, but I'm not entirely comfortable with it."

### Use "I" When Appropriate
- ❌ "It is recommended that..."
- ✅ "I recommend..."

### Be Specific About Feelings
- ❌ "This is concerning."
- ✅ "There's something unsettling about this."

## Example: Full Humanization

**Original (AI-generated):**
> The new dashboard represents a pivotal moment in our analytics evolution. Furthermore, it leverages cutting-edge visualization techniques to facilitate deeper insights. The interface is intuitive, robust, and seamless, underscoring our commitment to excellence. Many users have noted that it serves as a testament to our innovative approach.

**Humanized:**
> The new dashboard is actually useful. We spent way too long on the visualizations, but people seem to get it. The interface doesn't crash, which is nice. Look, I'm biased - I helped build this - but I keep catching myself using it instead of the old one. That probably means something.
